Conducted Studies/Research

It is not a secret that many military veterans especially those involved in war were prescribed opioids for chronic pain. The prevalence of opioid prescriptions among veterans increased from 18.9% to 33% from 2004-2012. . In a meta-analysis conducted among patients who had been prescribed opioids for chronic pain; ten predictors were used to see how high in relation they were to opioid overdoses. It was found that the mental health indicators for this study used bipolar disorder, depression, and other mental health disorders proved to be moderate indicators of opioid fatalities. However, another indicator was history of prescription of fentanyl and other opioids. This indicator had a high risk of association of resulting in opioid fatalities.

A cohort study that was done consisting of only veterans who had prior history of a non-fatal overdose compared to veterans who had no history of opioid overdose. This study identified 8370 Veterans with a history of nonfatal opioid overdose and a total follow-up of 39,207 person-years. The veterans that had history of prior opioid non-fatal overdose, approximately 17.4 % of them experienced death in the follow-up of the study
