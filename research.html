Opioids for Chronic Pain in Veterans

The prevalence of opioid prescriptions among veterans increased from 18.9% to 33% from 2004-2012.In a meta-analysis conducted among patients who had been prescribed opioids for chronic pain; ten predictors were used to see how high in relation they were to opioid overdoses. It was found that the mental health indicators for this study used bipolar disorder, depression, and other mental health disorders proved to be moderate indicators of opioid fatalities. However, another indicator was history of prescription of fentanyl and other opioids. This indicator had a high risk of association, that resulted in fatalities from opioids.     • Opioids and Mental Health

    According to (Warfield, Karras, Lilly, Brumage, & Bossarte, 2021), a cohort study was conducted consisting of only veterans who had prior history of a non-fatal overdose and veterans who had no history of opioid overdose. This study identified 8370 Veterans with a history of nonfatal opioid overdose and a total follow-up of 39,207 person-years. The veterans that had history of prior opioid non-fatal overdose, approximately 17.4 % of them experienced death in the follow-up of the study. In this study the most common causes of death amongst veterans were identified.Veterans who had history of non-fatal opioid overdose were two times more likely to die from suicide in comparison to the veteran group with no history of opioid overdose. About 30% of veterans who use VA services had a prevalence of overdose from opioids between 2001-2009. The rise quickly went up to 65% of the veteran population from 2010-2016
